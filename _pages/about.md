I am Wei Shi (施为), a Research Scientist at Meta working on **LLM post-training, causal learning, and reward modeling** — particularly the problem of building robust learning signals at large scale, where the signals are often confounded, sparse, and noisy, through a causal lens.

I obtained my Ph.D. at UT Austin under Prof. [David Z. Pan](http://www.ece.utexas.edu/~dpan/) (**AI for chip design**) and Prof. [Nan Sun](https://www.sunresearchgroup.top/zh/) (**high-performance mixed-signal design**). High-performance mixed-signal design demands a deep understanding of silicon physics, control theory, and signal processing, together with the engineering discipline to manage extreme complexity — where timing errors are measured in microseconds or tighter. AI for chip design must rise to those same ambitions: satisfying multi-dimensional objectives, capturing the implicit intuition of human experts, and executing designs at very high precision. The question I work on today first surfaced there: how to align learning systems with objectives that human experts hold mostly as tacit understanding.

As an ML enthusiast, chip designer, and anime fan, I've trained models, taped out silicon, and dabbled in drawing manga. AI is my most recent excitement — especially the rapidly advancing STEM reasoning capabilities of frontier LLMs and the emerging promise of autonomous AI research agents.
